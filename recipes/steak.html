Steak

Description

This pan seared steak has a garlic butter that makes it taset like a steakhouse quality meal! You'll be impressed at how easy it is to make the perfect steak that's seared on the outside, and perfectly tender inside.

Ingredients

  • 2 steaks (ribeye or NY strip)
  • 1/2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 1/2 sea salt
  • 1 tsp fresh ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary

Steps

  1. Bring steak to room temperature on countertop.
  2. Pat steak dry with paper towels. Season generously with 1 1/2 tsp salt and 1 tsp black pepper just before cooking.
  3. Heat a cast iron skillet on medium high heat until hot and add 1/2 tbsp of avocado oil to coat. Add steaks to the skill once hot. Sear the steaks on the first side for 4 minutes until a brown crust has formed, then flip and cook another 3-4 minutes. Using tongs, turn the steak on its sides to render the white fat and sear the edges (about 1 minutes per edge).
  4. Reduce heat to medium and immediately add 2 tbsp butter, minced garlic cloves, and rosemary to the skillet. Spoon the butter sauce over the steak, tilting the pan to get butter onto your spoon. Continue spooning the sauce over the steak for a minute or until the steak is about 5-10 degrees from your desired doneness (steaks will continue to cook as they rest).
  5. Transfer steak to a cutting board, loosely cover, and rest 10 minutes before slicing into 1/2" strips to serve. Spoon extra butter sauce over sliced steak to serve.
